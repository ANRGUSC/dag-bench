Distributed video analytics pipeline faithfully extracted from Figure 5 (Application 3) of Li et al. (arXiv 2024). Video data is read from storage, split into segments, then processed through two parallel branches of frame extraction and classification before results are written back to storage. A metadata/manifest edge from SplitVideo to WriteStorage completes the 8-edge structure. Evaluated on a real heterogeneous testbed with Desktop, Jetson AGX Xavier, Xavier NX, and TX2 devices.

Notes: DAG structure faithfully extracted from Figure 5 'DAG-based applications used in the experiments', specifically Application 3 (Video Analytics). The pipeline reads video from storage, splits into segments, extracts frames through two parallel branches, classifies each branch, and writes results. The bypass edge from SplitVideo to WriteStorage carries segment metadata. Task costs are estimated proportional to computational complexity (classification is heaviest, I/O is lightest). Data transfer sizes are estimated from typical video processing data dimensions. The paper evaluates on a real testbed with heterogeneous hardware (Table III): Desktop (16-core x86, 32GB), Jetson AGX Xavier (8-core ARM, 16GB), Xavier NX (6-core ARM, 8GB), Jetson TX2 (4-core ARM, 8GB).
