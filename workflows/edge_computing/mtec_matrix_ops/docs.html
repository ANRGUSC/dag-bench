Distributed matrix operations pipeline faithfully extracted from Figure 5 (Application 4) of Li et al. (arXiv 2024). A matrix is loaded then forked to transpose and multiply operations. Both feed into a matrix-vector multiplication, while the multiply result also feeds into matrix inversion. The inversion and first mat-vec multiply converge at a final mat-vec multiply, creating complex overlapping diamond structures. The most structurally interesting of the four M-TEC applications.

Notes: DAG structure faithfully extracted from Figure 5 'DAG-based applications used in the experiments', specifically Application 4 (Matrix Operations). The pipeline loads a matrix, then performs transpose and multiply in parallel. Both results feed into a matrix-vector multiplication, while the multiply also feeds into inversion. The final mat-vec multiply combines inversion and first mat-vec results. This creates overlapping diamond structures making it the most topologically interesting M-TEC app. Task costs are estimated proportional to computational complexity: O(n^3) operations (multiply, inversion) are heaviest, O(n^2) operations (transpose, mat-vec) moderate, I/O lightest.
